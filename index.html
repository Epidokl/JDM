About JDM

Japanese Domestic Marketis a common term for cars sold in the Japanese market. Typically, car models destined for Japan are different from the same models destined for other markets, or they do not have foreign analogues at all.

The usual period of car ownership in Japan is 10-18 years, rarely 20 years. Further, keeping an old car becomes economically unprofitable due to the strict requirements of state technical control, due to which cars, upon reaching this age, are sold very inexpensively, mainly at auctions specially organized for this purpose. A person from any state can become a buyer of such a car. The buyer sells the old car to the dealer at the residual value, and this value goes towards the down payment on the new one, so the buyer simply continues to pay off the loan.
